# LinkedIn Post — FP-22

I asked an AI agent to benchmark itself through a research governance pipeline.

45 conditions. 3 task complexities. 3 governance levels. 5 seeds.

The result that surprised me most:

Governance structure explains 4.4x more quality variance than task difficulty.

Translation: HOW you instruct an AI agent matters more than WHAT you ask it to do.

Key numbers:
- Full governance: 8.93/10 mean quality
- No governance: 5.47/10
- Improvement: +3.5 points (Cohen's d = 2.58)
- Gate 0 pass rate: 0% without templates, 100% with

The agent can fill any template you give it. The real challenge is the "last mile" — getting insightful analysis, not just complete sections.

Three takeaways for anyone using AI agents for research:
1. Structured governance templates are the biggest quality lever
2. Structure is solved; analytical depth is not
3. Invest in prompt frameworks, not simpler tasks
